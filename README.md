# Project-Sprint-7
TripleTen’s project for the seventh sprint, in which I analyzed trip data from a taxi app.

<h1>Analyst at Zuber</h1>
<h2>A new taxi app company has recently launched in Chicago, and the objective of this project was to identify patterns in the available data, such as passenger preferences and the impact of external factors on rides.</h2>

<p align="center">
  <img src="images/average_trips_tourist_locations.png" width="700">
</p>

<p>The most popular destinations among users are tourist attractions and the airport, suggesting that most taxi app users are likely tourists, since local residents would be more inclined to use public transportation or their own vehicles. It is also worth noting that these destinations are relatively close to one another.</p>

<p align="center">
  <img src="images/most_useds_company_cabs.png" width="700">
</p>

<p>It is noticeable that Flash Cab holds the largest market share. While most companies have a similar number of rides, Flash Cab has almost twice as many rides as the second-largest competitor.</p>

<p align="center">
  <img src="images/distribution_trips_duration.png" width="700">
</p>

<p>Based on the t-test results, along with the histogram analysis, we can observe that there is a statistically significant difference between trip durations on rainy and non-rainy days. As expected, drivers tend to be more cautious during rainy weather, reducing their speed and slightly increasing trip duration. Additionally, people are generally less likely to go out when it is raining, which is reflected in the graph, as rainy days show significantly fewer trips overall.</p>
